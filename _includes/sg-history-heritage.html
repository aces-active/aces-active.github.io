Singapore History & Heritage

Chinatown

A quiet enclave amid a former kampong with street identities, overseen by former Combined Ops HQ on Pearl's Hill.

Katong-Joo Chiat Art Circuit

Once a weekend retreat by city dwellers, now filled with pre-ware architecture - colourful shophouses, intricate motifs, peppered with murals and heritage boards.

Marina Bay Waterfront

Once a traditional important site for trade, the Singapore River was tranformed into a clean and pleasant destination lined by historic buildings.

Botanic Gardens

A cultural cornerstone and the first and only tropical garden on the list of UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Upper Peirce Reservoir

A major water supply project to support our rapid housing and industrialisation programme.

Green Corridor

Once a critical transport link between tiny Singapore to Eurasia, now reserved as an intricate network of micro-ecosystems.
