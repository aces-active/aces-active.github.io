History

Once filled with coconut plantations and used as a weekend retreat by wealthy city dwellers, Katong developed into a residential suburb by the early 20th century. It became populated by a growing English-educated middle class, including Peranakans and Eurasians. The neighbouring Joo Chiat area is named after Chew Joo Chiat, a wealthy Chinese landowner in the early 20th century. The area's identity is especially shaped by its unique pre-war architecture – colourful two-storey shophouses and terrace houses with ornate facades, intricate motifs and ceramic tiles. Also keep your eyes peeled for the various murals and heritage board that pepper this route, which will offer you a glimpse of the history behind Katong – Joo Chiat. Finally, come hungry as there are plenty of food options that Katong – Joo Chiat has to offer.
